One-Pot Spaghetti

Description

One-pot spaghetti is a simple and convenient cooking method where all ingredients—spaghetti, sauce, vegetables, and seasonings—are cooked together in a single pot. This technique allows the pasta to absorb flavors while cooking, resulting in a rich, cohesive dish. It’s quick to prepare and minimizes cleanup, making it a favorite for busy weeknight dinners. Just combine everything, bring it to a boil, simmer, and enjoy a delicious, satisfying meal!

Ingredients:

  • Spaghetti (12 ounces)
  • Olive oil (2 tablespoons)
  • Garlic (2-3 cloves, minced)
  • Onion (1, chopped)
  • Crushed tomatoes (1 can, 14 ounces)
  • Vegetable or chicken broth (4 cups)
  • Italian seasoning (1-2 teaspoons)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Fresh basil or parsley (for garnish)
  • Optional add-ins:
    • Vegetables (like bell peppers, spinach, or mushrooms)
    • Protein (like cooked chicken, shrimp, or sausage)
    • Red pepper flakes (for heat)

Feel free to customise based on your preferences

Steps:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until the onion is translucent.
  2. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, broth, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. If using vegetables or protein, add them now.
  3. Add the uncooked spaghetti, making sure it’s submerged in the liquid.
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Cook for about 10-12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the pasta is al dente and the liquid has mostly absorbed.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  6. Remove from heat and let it sit for a couple of minutes. Garnish with fresh basil or parsley before serving.

Enjoy your delicious one-pot spaghetti!
